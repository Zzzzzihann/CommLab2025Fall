Looking Ahead

Nuston: “We’re Ready — Even Manchester City Can Be Challenged.”

As FC Animalia prepares for their UEFA Champions League showdown against reigning 22/23’s champion Manchester City, our talented winger Viní Jr. Nutson exudes both respect and quiet confidence for the upcoming game.

    The energetic squirrel, known for its explosive pace and sharp dribbling down the right flank, knows what’s at stake — and who they’re up against.

    “No doubts, Man City are one of the best and toughest teams in the world,” Nutson admitted after training. “With players like Erling Haaland up front, you can’t afford a single mistake. One second of space, and he’ll punish you.”

    But despite acknowledging the challenge, Nutson believes FC Animalia has reasons to be optimistic. With City’s midfield anchor and the Ballon d'Or winner Rodri ruled out due to suspension, the winger sees potential openings in the half-spaces.

    “They’ll still be dangerous, but maybe that absence gives us a small chance to exploit the flanks and the channels,” he said. “We’ve trained hard on transitions — if we can move the ball quickly, we can hurt them.”

    Head Coach Clawson has emphasized compact defending and rapid counterattacks in recent sessions, with Nutson expected to play a key role in turning defense into offense. “We’re ready,” Nutson concluded, tail flicking with determination. “No one said it would be easy — but that’s exactly why we play.”

    It isn’t loud or theatrical — yet somehow, Meowssi has become the emotional core of this team. Renowned for its remarkable balance and low center of gravity, its dribbling often seems to defy physics, as though even gravity bends to its rhythm.

    FC Animalia will face Manchester City this Thursday at The Den Arena, in what promises to be one of the most anticipated clashes of the Champions League round.
