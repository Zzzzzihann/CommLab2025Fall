Raquinho Signs Three-Year Deal After Loan

“No Matter How Far the Ball Rolls, I’m Ready to Chase It”

When the news broke that Raquinho had signed a three-year contract with FC Animalia following his loan spell, The Den Arena erupted in celebration.


    For fans, this wasn’t just a contract announcement; it was the continuation of a warm, beloved story — the tale of the clever, spirited raccoon who has become one of the club’s most endearing figures.

    The 23-year-old raccoon, known for his lightning reflexes and signature grin, joined FC Animalia on loan from NYCFC in January 2025. In just half a season, he became one of the most adored players on the team — not only for his technical brilliance, but for the sheer joy he brings every time he “steals” the ball from an opponent’s feet.

    “I’m really happy,” Raquinho said after signing the new deal, flashing his trademark smile. “This club already feels like home. From the first day, I could feel the energy here — the noise, the fans, the smell of the grass at The Den. It’s a special place. I want to stay and make bigger stories here.”

      From Street Corners to Stadium Lights

    Born and raised in the backstreets of New York, Raquinho’s journey to professional football has been as unpredictable as his playing style. As a young raccoon, he weaved through traffic cones, sidestepped pedestrians, and chased the occasional ball rolling down the slope — those street instincts shaped the player he is today: a defensive midfielder with dazzling dribbling and powerful forward drive.

    “It’s funny,” he said with a laugh. “People say I play like I’m stealing something. Maybe they’re right — I do love stealing the ball. It’s just who I am.”


      “I May Not Be the Tallest, But I See the Clearest”

    Standing just under 1.2 meters tall, Raquinho doesn’t always stand out physically — but his anticipation and spatial awareness make him one of the sharpest defenders in the league. Last season, he averaged 4.1 successful tackles per match, several of which directly led to goals by Meowssi and Nutson.

    “I never worry about my height,” Raquinho said. “The ball doesn’t care either. What matters is whether you can see the moment — where the space is, where the danger is. You just have to see it a heartbeat earlier than everyone else.”

    It’s this blend of instinct and street-born intuition that makes Raquinho irreplaceable in the FC Animalia lineup.


        Beyond The Den

    With his contract extended through 2029, Raquinho’s goals are clear — to take FC Animalia to new heights. When asked about his ambitions for the coming season, his eyes sparkled with determination.

    “I want to win everything — the league, the Species Cup, maybe even the Champions League someday. But more than that, I want our fans to be proud of us. Every tackle, every sprint, every scratch mark — it’s all for them.”

     He recalled the first time he saw two young fans trading his player card outside the stadium — one clutching it like a treasure, the other smiling in triumph after the swap. “I just stood there watching,” he said, tail flicking with amusement. “That was when I realized how much this game means, not just to us on the field, but to everyone who believes in us.”

    As the sun set, casting a soft orange glow over The Den, Raquinho stayed behind on the pitch, juggling a ball with his tail before flicking it neatly into the goal. “Some habits,” he grinned, “you just can’t change.”
