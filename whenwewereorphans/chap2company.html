The Company

File No.04

• Morgan Brook & Byatt Company, established in 1820 and headquartered in London, originally focused on trade between the British Empire and British India.

• After the 1830s, the company expanded its operations into East and Southeast Asia, establishing offices in cities such as Tokyo, Shanghai, Tianjin, Hong Kong, and Singapore, where it engaged primarily in the exchange of goods from British India to Asia.

• The Shanghai branch employed approximately twenty-five staff members and was supervised by Harold Anderson.

• Its Chinese name was recorded as “Mogen Yanghang” (摩根洋行).
