Mom

File No.03

• Born in 1875 in Brighton, lost her parents at a young age and was subsequently raised by relatives in London.

• She met her future husband at a ball in 1896, and the two married the following year before relocating to Shanghai. Their only child, Christopher, was born in 1903 at the Shanghai French Hospital.

• During her years in Shanghai, Mrs. Banks was frequently seen in social and church circles within the International Settlement. She often hosted gatherings in her home and was regarded as an active organizer and advocate for various social initiatives.

• After her husband’s disappearance, people from different circles visited the house regularly to exchange news and offer information.

• One morning, while her son was out, she was abducted from the residence and subsequently vanished.

• People said that she might be traped in a white house in Shanghai.
